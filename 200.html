Solution · DFS Flood Fill (In-place Marking)
1
Scan grid: for every cell with '1', increment islandCount and immediately call dfs(grid, r, c) to sink the whole island.
2
DFS base case: return if out of bounds OR grid[row][col] == '0' — already water or already visited.
3
Sink & spread: mark current cell '0', then recurse in all 4 directions to flood-fill the entire connected land mass.
↑ dfs(row-1, col)
↓ dfs(row+1, col)
← dfs(row, col-1)
→ dfs(row, col+1)
Why mark '0' before recursing? Setting grid[row][col] = '0' immediately prevents revisiting — acts as an in-place visited set. No extra memory needed, and the outer scan will skip it on future iterations.
