Solution · Reverse DFS from Ocean Borders
1
Create two visited matrices, pacificReachable and atlanticReachable. These record which cells can reach each ocean.
2
Start DFS from all Pacific border cells, top row and left column, and from all Atlantic border cells, bottom row and right column.
🌊 DFS move condition
next cell is inside grid, not visited, and heights[newRow][newCol] >= heights[row][col] continue DFS
out of bounds, already visited, or lower height skip
3
After both DFS passes, scan the grid. If pacificReachable[i][j] and atlanticReachable[i][j] are both true, add [i, j] to the result.
4
Why it works, water normally flows downhill, so in reverse search we only move to cells with greater or equal height. That tells us which cells can eventually flow to that ocean.
Core insight: do not start DFS from every cell. Start from each ocean border and move backward into the land using non-decreasing heights. The overlap of the two reachable sets gives the answer.
