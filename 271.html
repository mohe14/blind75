Encode & Decode Strings

Design an algorithm to encode a list of strings to a single string, and decode it back — even if strings contain any character including #.

input    ["lint", "code", "love", "you"]
encoded 4#lint4#code4#love3#you
decoded ["lint", "code", "love", "you"]

Solution · Length-Prefixed Encoding
encode()
decode()
1
Encode: for each string, append len + "#" + str to a StringBuffer. The length tells the decoder exactly how many chars to read.
4#lint 4#code 4#love 3#you
2
Decode — find splitter: from pos, call indexOf("#", pos) to locate the next #.
3
Parse length: parseInt(s.substring(pos, splitterIndex)) gives the exact word length.
4
Extract word: s.substring(splitterIndex+1, splitterIndex+1+len), add to list, then advance pos past the word.
★
Why not just split on "#"? Strings may contain # themselves. The length prefix makes decoding unambiguous — no delimiter conflicts possible.
